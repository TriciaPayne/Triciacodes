I wanted to share my story about becoming a Techmom! I was a stay at home mom for 11 years. I was married to a Tech-guy. We got divorced and it was hard to provide for myself. I tried a lot of different entry level jobs and nothing really was a great fit. I didn't feel valued and like I had any valuable gifts to contribute to the world.

I found the link to the Techmoms website one day when I was scrolling on a Social Media app. I read the website and thought it would be so cool to have a supportive group of women to help me try to establish myself in a respectable career and to cheer me on all along the way. I got excited about it. Then the voices of doubt came into my mind and told me all of the reasons why it couldn't work out. Among them I heard messages like "you have to be there for your kids" and "you don't have what it takes to have a successful IT career". A lot of messages went through my mind and I was convinced that I would not be able to do it.

After several days passed, I thought about how happy it made me to think about doing coding and having this group of women to support me. I felt like I'd better sign up! So I did. I got more discouraging voices in my head. It took me a lot of prayer and thought work before I just made up my mind and went forward. During the first class I shared my story and it was great. I started to realize that all of the other women were just like me in some way. I wasn't alone trying to figure this world out. I had friends now and people to help me learn these valuable new skills. It was a journey.

Through my journey I learned to build a website and I learned to believe in myself and I learned that I can do hard things and I don't have to settle for any of the beliefs or lack of belief that anyone had ever given me. There are people who are here to support me with resources of all kinds and I feel hope and joy in my journey of learning!
